Smart Footballs

These footballs have sensors built into them that track metrics such as distance thrown, spin rate, and spiral efficiency. This technology can be used to help players improve their throwing mechanics and help coaches make more informed decisions about player development.

Wearable GPS Tracking Devices

These devices can be worn by athletes during training or games and provide real-time data on metrics such as distance covered, speed, and acceleration. This technology can be used to optimize training and improve performance on game day.

Virtual Reality Training Systems

These systems use VR headsets and other specialized equipment to create immersive training environments for athletes. For example, a basketball player might use a VR system to practice shooting free throws or a soccer player might use it to practice penalty kicks. This technology can be used to supplement traditional training methods and help athletes develop new skills.
